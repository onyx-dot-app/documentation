Skills and apps are how Craft's agent loop reaches beyond plain text generation.

- **Skills** teach Craft a repeatable method or specialized capability, like building a slide deck or searching
  your company knowledge.
- **Apps** connect external services so Craft can read from and act in the tools your team already uses.

Together they turn a single prompt into real work.
The all-hands deck builds on the `pptx` skill to format slides and the `company-search` skill to ground them in real
numbers. A customer report reads HubSpot, Slack, and Zendesk through connected apps before it writes a word.

## Skills

A skill is a reusable bundle of instructions and supporting files that teaches Craft how to do a specific kind of work:
searching company knowledge, building a deck, or generating an image, for example.

<img className="rounded-image" src="/assets/overview/core_features/craft_skills.png" alt="The Craft Skills page listing available built-in and personal skills"/>

You don't pick skills manually. **Craft selects the skills a request needs on its own**, based on what each one is for.
To steer it toward a particular skill, open the **+** menu and choose **Skills**, or type **/** to search by name.
That surfaces the skill for the current request; Craft still decides how to apply it.

Skills come from three places:

- **Built-in skills** ship with Onyx, including company search, slide generation, and image generation.
- **Organization skills** are uploaded by admins and shared with everyone or with specific groups.
- **Personal skills** are uploaded by you and visible only to you.

What you see depends on your workspace configuration and what your admin has enabled.

<Note>
  Some built-in skills depend on a configured provider. Image generation, for example,
  needs an admin to set up an image model. If a skill you expect is missing,
  see [why a skill or app may not appear](#why-something-may-not-appear).
</Note>

## Apps

Connect an app when Craft needs to read from or act in an external service. Built-in app types include **Slack,
Google Calendar, Google Drive, Gmail, Linear, and GitHub**,
and your organization can add custom apps for internal systems.

<img className="rounded-image" src="/assets/overview/core_features/craft_apps.png" alt="The Craft Apps page showing connected apps and apps available to connect"/>

Open the **Apps** page from the Craft sidebar (or `/craft/v1/apps`) to connect one:

- **Built-in apps** connect through OAuth. You sign in to the service and grant access.
- **Custom apps** may ask for credential fields defined by your admin.

Once connected, Craft can use the app on any request. Point it at a specific app from the **+** menu under **Apps**,
or just describe the task ("post the summary to the #launch Slack channel").

<Info>
  Your app credentials never enter Craft's sandbox. When Craft makes a request,
  Onyx injects the real credential into the approved outbound call on your behalf.
  See [Craft Architecture](/security/architecture/craft) for the full security model.
</Info>

## Approvals

Reading is one thing; acting is another. Some app actions need your approval before Craft can continue,
typically anything that sends, creates, edits, or deletes. When that happens,
an approval card appears in the chat with the app, the action, and the exact request Craft wants to send.

<img className="rounded-image" src="/assets/overview/core_features/craft_approval_card.png" alt="A Craft approval card requesting approval to post a message in Slack, with Approve once, Approve for session, and Reject buttons"/>

| Choice | Use it when |
| --- | --- |
| **Approve once** | You trust this exact action but don't want to allow similar ones automatically. |
| **Approve for session** | Craft needs to repeat this kind of action in the current session and the scope is clear. |
| **Reject** | The action is wrong, too broad, or shouldn't run. |

Two things to know:

- **Approvals expire.** If you don't respond in time, the action fails closed and does not run.
- **Approvals are scoped to the session.** *Approve for session* does not change your organization's app policy
  or create a lasting permission.

### Approve safely

Before you approve, check:

- Which app Craft is using.
- Whether the action only reads or actually changes something.
- Which message, issue, event, file, or endpoint is involved.
- Whether it matches what you asked for.

Prefer **Approve once** for sends, deletes, edits, calendar changes, or anything that touches a customer-facing system.

## Skills and apps in Scheduled Tasks

A [Scheduled Task](/overview/core_features/craft_scheduled_tasks) runs unattended,
so a live approval prompt would stall it. When a task needs an app,
**pre-approve that app** in the task configuration so it can act on its own. Keep pre-approval to narrowly scoped tasks,
and avoid pre-approving apps that can send, delete, or change sensitive records.
If a run hits an action that still needs approval, it stops in an *awaiting approval* state instead of finishing.

## Why something may not appear

A skill or app might be missing because:

- An admin hasn't enabled it.
- It's shared only with a different group.
- You haven't connected the app yet.
- The app is connected but its OAuth or credential setup is incomplete.
- A personal skill failed validation when it was uploaded.

Admins manage organization skills, apps, and approval policies from the Craft admin pages.
See [Managing Craft](/admins/managing_features/craft).
