Skills and apps extend what Craft can do. **Skills** teach Craft a repeatable method or specialized capability.
**Apps** let Craft read from and act in external services your organization has connected.

## Skills

A skill is a reusable bundle of instructions and supporting files that teaches Craft how to do a specific kind of work —
searching your company knowledge, building a slide deck, or generating an image, for example.

<img className="rounded-image" src="/assets/overview/core_features/craft_skills.png" alt="The Craft Skills page listing available built-in skills"/>

You don't have to pick a skill manually. **Craft chooses the relevant skills for your request automatically**,
based on what each skill is for. If you want to steer it toward a particular skill,
open the **+** menu and choose **Skills**, or type **/** in the input to search by name.
This surfaces the skill for the current request — Craft still decides how to apply it.

Skills come from a few places:

- **Built-in skills** — shipped with Onyx, such as company knowledge search, presentations, and image generation.
- **Organization skills** — uploaded by your admins and shared with everyone or with specific groups.
- **Personal skills** — uploaded by you and visible only to you.

Which skills you see depends on your workspace configuration and what your admin has enabled.

<Note>
  Some built-in skills depend on a configured provider. For example,
  image generation is only available when an admin has set up an image model. If a skill you expect is missing,
  see [why a skill or app may not appear](#why-something-may-not-appear).
</Note>

## Apps

Connect an app when Craft needs to read from or act in an external service. Built-in app types include **Slack,
Google Calendar, Google Drive, Gmail, Linear, and GitHub**.
Your organization may also add custom apps for internal systems.

<img className="rounded-image" src="/assets/overview/core_features/craft_apps.png" alt="The Craft Apps page showing connected apps and apps available to connect"/>

Open the **Apps** page from the Craft sidebar (or `/craft/v1/apps`) to connect one:

- **Built-in apps** connect through OAuth — you'll sign in to the service and grant access.
- **Custom apps** may ask for credential fields defined by your admin.

Once an app is connected, Craft can use it. Surface a specific app for a request from the **+** menu under **Apps**,
or just describe the task ("post the summary to the #launch Slack channel").

<Info>
  Your app credentials never enter Craft's sandbox. When Craft makes a request,
  Onyx injects the real credential into the approved outbound call on your behalf.
  See [Craft Architecture](/security/architecture/craft) for the full security model.
</Info>

## Approvals

Some app actions need your approval before Craft can continue — typically anything that sends, creates, edits,
or deletes. When that happens, an approval card appears in the chat showing the app, the action,
and the request details Craft wants to send.

<img className="rounded-image" src="/assets/overview/core_features/craft_approval_card.png" alt="A Craft approval card requesting approval to post a message in Slack, with Approve once, Approve for session, and Reject buttons"/>

| Choice | Use it when |
| --- | --- |
| **Approve once** | You trust this exact action but don't want to allow similar ones automatically. |
| **Approve for session** | Craft needs to repeat this kind of action in the current session and the scope is clear. |
| **Reject** | The action is wrong, unexpected, too broad, or shouldn't run. |

A few things to know:

- **Approvals expire.** If you don't respond in time, the action fails closed (it does not run).
- **Approvals are scoped to the session.** *Approve for session* does not change your organization's app policy or
  create a lasting permission.

### Approve safely

Before approving, check:

- Which app Craft is using.
- Whether the action only reads, or changes something.
- Which message, issue, event, file, or endpoint is involved.
- Whether it matches what you actually asked for.

Prefer **Approve once** for sends, deletes, edits, calendar changes, or anything that touches a customer-facing system.

## Skills and apps in Scheduled Tasks

A [Scheduled Task](/overview/core_features/craft_scheduled_tasks) runs without you watching the chat,
so a live approval prompt would stall it. When a task needs an app,
**pre-approve that app** in the task configuration so it can act unattended.

Pre-approve only narrowly scoped tasks, and avoid pre-approving apps that can send, delete, or change sensitive records.
If a scheduled run hits an action that still needs approval,
the run stops in an *awaiting approval* state instead of completing.

## Why something may not appear

A skill or app might be missing because:

- An admin hasn't enabled it.
- It's shared only with a different group.
- You haven't connected the app yet.
- The app is enabled but its OAuth or credential setup is incomplete.
- A personal skill failed validation when it was uploaded.

Admins manage organization skills, apps, and approval policies from the Craft admin pages.
See [Managing Craft](/admins/managing_features/craft).
